# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

Be kind. Be helpful. Assume good intentions.

This is a small project built by someone who believes marketing shouldn't need a massive budget. If you're here, you probably believe that too. Let's keep it that way.

## What We Expect

- Be respectful. Disagree with ideas, not people.
- Be constructive. "This doesn't work" is less helpful than "This doesn't work because X — maybe try Y?"
- Be welcoming. Somebody's first contribution might not be perfect. Neither was yours. Help them get there.
- Be patient. This is open source — people contribute in their free time, across different time zones and skill levels.

## What We Won't Tolerate

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks — in issues, PRs, discussions, or DMs related to this project
- Discrimination of any kind
- Spam, self-promotion disguised as contributions, or trolling
- Publishing others' private information without permission

## Reporting Issues

If someone's behavior makes you uncomfortable, reach out to the maintainer directly. Reports will be reviewed and handled privately. Nobody who reports a concern in good faith will face retaliation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will address violations as they see fit — a private conversation, a warning, a temporary ban, or a permanent removal depending on severity. The goal is to keep this community productive and welcoming.

## Scope

This applies everywhere this project lives: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, related social media threads, and any communication tied to contributions.
