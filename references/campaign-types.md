# Campaign Type Playbooks

Step-by-step content sequences for different campaign types. Each playbook defines what to post, when, and why — so the AI agent can generate the right content in the right order.

---

## 🚀 Product Launch Campaign

A structured 14-day sequence to build anticipation, launch with impact, and sustain momentum.

### Pre-Launch Phase (Days -7 to -1)

| Day | Post Type | Purpose | Platform Focus |
|-----|-----------|---------|----------------|
| -7 | **Mystery teaser** | Spark curiosity without revealing anything | Instagram Story, Twitter |
| -6 | **Problem post** | Highlight the pain point your product solves | LinkedIn, Instagram |
| -5 | **Behind-the-scenes** | Show the making-of, build authenticity | Instagram, TikTok |
| -4 | **Sneak peek** | Partial reveal — a UI element, a feature name, a screenshot with blur | Instagram, Twitter |
| -3 | **Social proof setup** | Share beta tester quote or early access testimonial | LinkedIn, Instagram |
| -2 | **Full reveal** | Show the product clearly for the first time | All platforms |
| -1 | **Countdown** | "Tomorrow" hype post with specific launch time | All platforms, Stories |

### Launch Day (Day 0)

| Timing | Post Type | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Morning | **Main announcement** | The big reveal post — hero image, key value prop, link | 
| Midday | **Feature breakdown** | Carousel or thread walking through top features |
| Afternoon | **Founder's note** | Personal story about why this was built — humanize |
| Evening | **Launch day recap** | Thank the community, share early numbers/reactions |
| All day | **Stories/Updates** | Real-time updates, milestone celebrations, user reactions |

### Post-Launch Phase (Days +1 to +7)

| Day | Post Type | Purpose |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| +1 | **Feature deep-dive #1** | Detailed look at the most exciting feature |
| +2 | **User reaction roundup** | Compile and share early user feedback |
| +3 | **Feature deep-dive #2** | Second feature spotlight |
| +4 | **"How to get started"** | Educational post lowering the barrier to try |
| +5 | **Comparison post** | Why this is different from alternatives (subtle, not aggressive) |
| +7 | **Week 1 results** | Share metrics, milestones, community size, early wins |

### Image Generation Notes for Launch Campaigns
- **Teasers:** Use blurred backgrounds, silhouettes, strategic crops that hide most of the product
- **Reveal posts:** Dramatic, high-impact — think product photography lighting
- **Feature posts:** Clean, focused — one feature per image, no clutter
- **Social proof:** Elegant quote cards matching brand style
- **Countdown:** Bold numbers, urgency colors (accent color), animated feel

## 📅 Always-On / Evergreen Campaign

For brands that need a sustainable, repeatable content rhythm. This is the default mode for ongoing social media management.

### Weekly Rhythm Template

```
Monday    → [Pillar 1] — Start the week strong (educational / value)
Tuesday   → [Pillar 2] — Thought leadership / industry insights
Wednesday → [Pillar 3] — Product spotlight / feature highlight
Thursday  → [Pillar 4] — Social proof / customer stories
Friday    → [Pillar 5] — Fun / behind-the-scenes / culture
Saturday  → REST or Story-only (light engagement)
Sunday    → REST or Content preview for next week
```

### Monthly Themes
Layer monthly themes on top of the weekly rhythm:
- **Month 1:** Brand awareness (who we are, what we stand for)
- **Month 2:** Education (become the go-to resource in your niche)
- **Month 3:** Social proof (let customers do the talking)
- **Month 4:** Product depth (show every feature, use case, benefit)
- **Repeat with seasonal variations**

### Refresh Cadence
- Swap out pillar emphasis every 4-6 weeks based on performance
- Introduce one new content format per month (e.g., memes, polls, AMAs)
- Re-audit competitors quarterly

## 🎄 Seasonal / Event Campaign

For holidays, industry events, conferences, Black Friday, New Year, etc.

### Timeline Template

| Phase | Timing | Content Type |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Warm-up** | 2 weeks before | Themed teasers, event countdown |
| **Build-up** | 1 week before | Exclusive previews, early-bird offers |
| **Peak** | Event day(s) | Main event content, live coverage, offers |
| **Wind-down** | 2-3 days after | Recap, thank you posts, last-chance offers |
| **Reflect** | 1 week after | Results shared, lessons learned, community appreciation |

### Visual Approach for Seasonal Campaigns
- Keep brand identity recognizable but add seasonal flavor
- Use seasonal color accents (don't abandon brand palette entirely)
- Themed overlays, frames, or borders rather than complete style changes
- Limited-edition feel — "this is special, not everyday"

## 🔥 Engagement / Growth Campaign

Focused on growing followers and building community, not direct sales.

### Tactics

| Tactic | Format | Frequency | Platform |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Ask Me Anything** | Story Q&A or live | 1x/month | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| **This or That** | Story poll | 2x/week | Instagram Stories |
| **User-Generated Content** | Repost/feature customers | 1x/week | Instagram, TikTok |
| **Giveaway** | Entry via engagement | 1x/month | Instagram, Twitter |
| **Collaboration** | Co-post with complementary brand | 2x/month | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| **Trending topic take** | Brand's angle on trending news | As relevant | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| **Community spotlight** | Feature a customer/user | 1x/week | All platforms |

## 🆘 Crisis Response Framework

When something goes wrong — outage, PR issue, negative viral moment.

### Response Sequence

1. **Acknowledge (within 1 hour)**
   - Short, honest post: "We're aware of [issue] and are working on it."
   - No defensive language. No excuses. Just facts and empathy.

2. **Update (ongoing)**
   - Regular updates on Stories/Twitter as situation develops
   - Pin the acknowledgment post so newcomers see it first

3. **Resolution (when fixed)**
   - Clear post explaining what happened and what was done
   - Thank the community for patience
   - Share what will prevent it in the future

4. **Recovery (1-2 weeks later)**
   - Slowly return to normal content schedule
   - Consider a "making it right" gesture (discount, feature, etc.)
   - Address it once more if asked, then move forward

### Tone During Crisis
- **DO:** Be human, be honest, be fast, be empathetic
- **DON'T:** Be defensive, go silent, blame others, delete comments, joke about it
