# Caption Writing Frameworks

Proven caption formulas that work across platforms. Fill in the blanks with brand-specific content. Each framework includes the psychology behind it and when to use it.

---

## 1. The Hook → Value → CTA (Universal Framework)

Best for: Any post, any platform. This is the default.

```
[Hook — one sentence that stops the scroll]

[2-3 sentences of real value — the "meat"]

[CTA — specific action for the reader]

[Hashtags if applicable]
```

**Example:**

```
Your competitors are posting 5x a week. You're posting once a month. 😬

Here's the thing — consistency beats perfection. Every single time.
The brands winning on social aren't the ones with the biggest budgets.
They're the ones who show up, even when it's not perfect.

What's your current posting frequency? Be honest 👇

#ContentStrategy #SocialMediaTips #MarketingAdvice
```

**Why it works:** The hook creates tension. The value provides relief. The CTA creates interaction.

## 2. The Problem → Agitate → Solve (PAS)

Best for: Product posts, educational content, pain-point-driven industries.

```
[State the problem the audience faces]

[Agitate — make them FEEL the problem. Consequences, frustrations, what they're missing]

[Present your solution — how your product/service/tip fixes this]

[CTA]
```

**Example:**

```
You spend 3 hours making a social media post. It gets 12 likes.
Your intern gets 10K views on a meme they made in 5 minutes.

It's not about effort. It's about strategy. Most brands create
content for themselves, not for their audience. They post what
THEY think is cool instead of what their followers actually need.

We built [Product] to fix exactly this — AI that understands your
audience and creates content they actually want to engage with.

Try it free → link in bio

#MarketingTools #SocialMediaMarketing
```

## 3. The Story Arc

Best for: Personal branding, behind-the-scenes, brand stories, LinkedIn.

```
[Setting — when/where this happened]

[Conflict — what went wrong or what challenge appeared]

[Turning point — the insight, decision, or moment of change]

[Resolution — what happened as a result]

[Lesson — the takeaway for the reader]

[CTA — usually a question]
```

**Example:**

```
Two years ago, I was a developer who hated marketing.

I'd built this beautiful product — spent 8 months coding it,
designing it, making it perfect. Then I launched it on Product
Hunt and... crickets. 47 views. 2 upvotes (one was my mom).

That day I realized: building a great product isn't enough.
You have to learn to TALK about it.

So I did. I started posting. Badly at first. But consistently.
Six months later, that same product had 10,000 users — all from
social media.

Your product deserves to be seen. Don't let it die in silence.

What's stopping you from posting? Drop it below 👇
```

## 4. The List Format

Best for: Tips, tools, resources, value-packed posts that get saved/shared.

```
[X] [Topic/Category] that [benefit/outcome]:

1️⃣ [Item 1] — [brief why]
2️⃣ [Item 2] — [brief why]
3️⃣ [Item 3] — [brief why]
4️⃣ [Item 4] — [brief why]
5️⃣ [Item 5] — [brief why]

Which one are you trying first?

[Hashtags]
```

**Example:**

```
5 free tools that replaced my $500/month marketing stack:

1️⃣ Canva — Design that doesn't look DIY
2️⃣ Buffer — Scheduling that actually works
3️⃣ AnswerThePublic — Content ideas when you're stuck
4️⃣ Unsplash — Photos that don't scream "stock"
5️⃣ [Your Product] — AI campaigns from your codebase 😉

Save this for later 🔖 You'll thank me.

#FreeTools #MarketingStack #StartupTools
```

## 5. The Bold Claim

Best for: Thought leadership, debate starters, engagement drivers. Use on Twitter/X and LinkedIn.

```
[Bold, potentially controversial statement]

[2-3 sentences backing it up with reasoning]

[Acknowledge the counter-argument briefly]

[Reaffirm your position with nuance]

[Ask for their take]
```

**Example:**

```
Most social media "strategies" are just vibes with a calendar.

Real strategy means knowing WHY you're posting each piece. What
business goal does it serve? What audience need does it meet?
What competitor gap does it fill?

Sure, consistency matters. But consistent mediocrity is still mediocrity.

Before your next post, ask: "Would I stop scrolling for this?"

Agree or disagree? Let's debate 💬
```

## 6. The Transformation

Best for: Before/after posts, case studies, product results, testimonials.

```
[Before state — the "old way" or "without us"]
↓
[After state — the "new way" or "with us"]

[How the transformation happened — keep it brief]

[Social proof element — numbers, quotes, timeline]

[CTA]
```

---

## 7. The Question Lead

Best for: Polls, engagement posts, community building, Stories.

```
[Question that hits a nerve — something your audience has strong opinions about]

[Brief context or your take — optional]

[Poll options or "Comment your answer"]
```

---

## 8. The Micro-Value Drop

Best for: Twitter/X, quick Instagram captions, daily tips.

```
[One actionable insight in 1-2 sentences]

[Optional: Why this matters in 1 sentence]

[CTA or hashtag]
```

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

### Instagram

- Front-load the hook — first 125 characters show before "more"
- Use line breaks between paragraphs (no walls of text)
- Put hashtags in a comment OR after line breaks at the bottom
- Emojis as visual anchors, not decoration

### LinkedIn

- First 2 lines are critical — they determine if people click "see more"
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each) for mobile readability
- Professional but not corporate — personal stories outperform
- No hashtags inline — put 3-5 at the bottom
- Tag relevant people and companies (when genuine, not spammy)

### Twitter / X

- Every word counts at 280 characters
- Threads for long-form: each tweet should work standalone AND as part of the thread
- First tweet of a thread is the hook — if it doesn't slap, nobody reads tweet 2
- Quote tweets > replies for visibility

### TikTok

- Captions support the video, they don't replace it
- First 3 words must be compelling (they appear on the thumbnail)
- Use trending sounds and effects when they fit the brand
- Reply to comments as new content

## Hook Templates (Fill-in-the-blank)

Use these when you're stuck on how to start:

| Type | Template |
|------|----------|
| **Curiosity** | "Nobody talks about [hidden truth about industry]..." |
| **Challenge** | "I dare you to [action] for [time period]. Here's what happens:" |
| **Myth-bust** | "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why:" |
| **Numbers** | "[Impressive stat] — and most [audience] have no idea." |
| **FOMO** | "While you were [common activity], [competitor/leader] was [winning action]." |
| **Confession** | "I'll be honest — I used to [mistake]. Then I learned..." |
| **Authority** | "After [big number] [thing done], here's what I know for sure:" |
| **Contrast** | "[Good thing] vs. [Bad thing] — which side are you on?" |
| **Trend** | "[Industry] is changing. Here's what [year] actually looks like:" |
| **Relatable** | "POV: You're a [role] who just [relatable situation]" |
